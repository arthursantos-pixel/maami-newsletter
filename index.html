For years, we swept the best colours onto the floor.

Cutting marble makes dust. Slurry, offcuts, fragments too small to sell. In most workshops, ours included, all of it used to leave on the back of a truck.

One day we stopped. We began grinding it, mixing it, pressing it back into something solid, and we called it Stonesis. It still carries the marble it came from. Only now we choose the colour.

Barrocal, Brick, Forest, Lisbon, Tivoli. Ten so far, each one its own mood, none of them quite the same twice.

We carve it slowly, one piece at a time, so nothing comes out identical. You choose the colour, the stone does the rest.

Nothing here was quarried twice.
